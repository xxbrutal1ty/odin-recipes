chicken_parmigiana

Description

A recipe for chicken parmigiana that I found on bonappetit.com

Ingredients

Tomato sauce

  • 1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 8 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 2 Tbsp. double-concentrated tomato paste
  • 3/4 tsp. crushed red pepper flakes
  • 2 28-oz. cans tomato puree
  • 2 tsp. Diamond Crystal or 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp. sugar

Chicken and Assembly

  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken breasts
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp. Diamond Crystal or 1 tsp. Morton kosher salt, plus more
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 Tbsp. garlic powder
  • 1 Tbsp. onion powder
  • 4 cups panko (Japanese breadcrumbs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 12 oz. low-moisture mozzeralla
  • 8 oz. pre-grated Parmesan
  • 2 Tbsp. finely chopped parsley

Steps

Tomato sauce

  1. Heat oil in large Dutch oven or other heavy pot over medium
  2. Add onion and garlic and cook, stirring often, until softened but not browned, about 5 minutes
  3. Add tomato paste and red pepper flakes and cook, stirring, until paste is slightly darkened in color (it should start to fry in oil), about 1 minute
  4. Add tomato puree, salt, and sugar, and bring to a simmer
  5. Partially cover pot with a lid (to avoid splattering), reduce heat so sauce is at a very bare simmer, and cook, stirring occasionally, until slightly thickened, about 40 to 50 minutes
  6. Sauce can be made 2 days ahead. Let cool. Transfer to airtight container; cover and let chill

    7. Chicken and Assembly

    1. Working one at a time, place chicken breast on a cutting board so short side is towards you and holding a chef's knife parallel to the breast, slice along the middle of long side to make a slit
    2. Continue to slice until you are about 1/2" from the other side
    3. Open breast up like a book and place between 2 sheets of wax paper or plastic wrap
    4. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, pound until 1/3" thick (not keeping it super thin will keep it from overcooking)
    5. Combine garlic, lemon juice, and olive oil in large baking dish
    6. Season chicken cutlets all over with salt (about 3/4 tsp. Diamond Crystal or 1/2 tsp. Morton kosher salt per cutlet)
    7. Add to marinade and turn to coat
    8. Let sit at least 20 min up to 1 hour
    9. Make a dredging stating: Whisk eggs, garlic powder, onion powder, 1/2 tsp. Diamond Crystal or Morton kosher salt, and 2 Tbsp. of water in a large shallow bowl
    10. Place panko in another large shallow bowl and flour in a third
    11. Whisk remaining 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or 1/2 tsp. Morton kosher salt into a flour
    12. Working with one cutlet at a time and letting any excess materials drip back into baking dish, dredge cutlets in flour, knocking off excess, then dip into egg wash, letting excess drip back into bowl
    13. Dredge in panko, pressing firmly to adhere, ensuring no bare spots remain
    14. Gently shake off excess and place cutlets on a rimmed baking sheet
    15. Chill at least 20 minutes and up to 8 hours
    16. Set a wire rack inside a second large rimmed baking sheet
    17. Grate mozzarella on the large holes of a box grater into a medium bowl; add Parmesan and toss to combine
    18. Pour vegetable oil into a large high-sided heavy skillet to come 1" up sides
    19. Heat over medium until an instant-read thermometer registers 400*
    20. Working in batches to avoid crowding the pan and returning oil to 400* after each batch, very carefully lower cutlets into skillet with tongs and cook until deep golden brown, about 2 minutes per side
    21. Transfer cutlets to prepared rack; season with salt
    22. Heat broiler
    23. Arrange cutlets side by side in 2 large baking dishes or separately in 4 smaller baking dishes
    24. Generously spoon some sauce over each cutlet (you want to mostly cover them but allow some corners and edges to remain uncovered)
    25. Spoon remaining sauce into baking dishes around the cutlets
    26. Cover cutlets with cheese mixture (again, leaving some of those cripsy edges uncovered)
    27. Broil until cheese is melted, bubbling, and browned in spots, about 4 minutes
    28. Remove the chicken from the broiler
    29. Let cool slightly and sprinkle with parsley
