banana-pancakes

Description

A recipe for banana pancakes that I found on loveandlemons.com

Ingredients

  • Bananas, of course, The spottier, the better. The riper your bananas are, the sweeter, moistier, and more flaverful your pancakes will be.
  • All-purpose flour - spoon and level the flour to avoid packing too much into your measuring cup.
  • Baking powder, baking sugar, and an egg - They help the pancakes fluff up as they cook.
  • Cane sugar - It brings out the sweetness of the bananas.
  • Almond milk - Or any milk you like.
  • Avocado oil - You'll add some to the pancake batter and use more for the pan. If you don't keep avocado oil on hand, feel free to substitute vegetable oil or melted butter.
  • Cinnamon, nutmeg, and vanilla extract - They add warm, spiced depth or flavor that makes these pancakes taste just like banana bread.
  • Sea salt - To make all the flavors pop.

Steps

  1. Whisk together the wet ingredients in one bowl and the dry ingredients in another
  2. Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients and fold until just combined
  3. Careful not to overmix, if you do the pancakes will be dense
  4. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-low heat, and pour 1/3 cup batter for each pancake into the hot skillet
  5. Cook for 1 to 2 minutes of the first side, flip the pancake, then cook for another 1 to 2 minutes.
  6. The pancakes are ready when they're puffed, golden brown, and cooked through
  7. If the pancakes are browning on the outside before they're done on the inside, turn down the heat
